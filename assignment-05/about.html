Lloyd Alexander

Born in 1924, Lloyd Alexander grew up in Pennsylvania during the Great Depression and later served in the US Army during World War II. Alexander's breakthrough
as an author wouldn't occur until 1955 after he wrote the autobiography And Let the Credit Go. He continued to find success publishing commissioned, non-fiction
works, until finally transitioning into writing children's fantasy books in the 1960's. After publishing nearly 50 books and earning numerous awards, Lloyd Alexander
passed away in 2007.
