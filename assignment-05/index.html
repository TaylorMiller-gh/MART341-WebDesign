The Black Cauldron

Lloyd Alexander

In a mythical kingdom of the Middle Ages, a young boy named Taran is forced into an adventure after the Horned King steals Taran's propheysing
pig, Hen-Wren. With the help of his friends, Taran must reach the magical Black Cauldron to stop the Horned King from summoning an undead army.

The Black Cauldron is the second book of The Chronicles of Pyrdain series. As part of the children's fantasy genre, the book seeks to impart fundamental
lessons such honesty, loyalty, and friendship in an easily consumable format. The book was published in 1965 and adpated into a film by Disney in 1985.
